The Story

The samosa didn't start in India.
It walked here.

It travelled from Central Asia, in the 13th century, in the saddlebags of Persian caravans. Then it stopped. It never left.

13th century · Central Asia

A pastry called sambusa travels with Persian caravans.

14th century · Delhi

Alauddin Khilji's chefs make it royal food.

17th–19th century · UP

Halwais claim it. Add potato. Add masala. Make it street.

20th century · Allahabad

Chole meets samosa in the gullies. The perfect breakfast is born.

2027 · Mumbai

Mishra Ji arrives.

By the time chole met samosa in Allahabad, it was 700 years old.
We're just the next stop.

Meet Mishra Ji

He's not real.
But every Mishra Ji from Prayagraj to Kanpur is.

He's the halwai who wakes at 4 AM so college kids have breakfast by 8. His hands know the exact snap of a well-fried samosa. His chole has been simmering since 5.

You've probably eaten at his stall. You just didn't know his name.

"I grew up eating his samosas. When I moved to Mumbai, I couldn't find them.
So we brought Mishra Ji here."

The Chole Samosa Ritual

Two samosas.
Cracked open.

01

Fried on order. Not sitting in a tray. Not warmed under a lamp. Fresh into hot oil the moment you say yes.

02

Chole since 5 AM. Simmered slow with UP masala. Tempered fresh with ghee and cumin the moment it's plated.

03

The bowl. Chutneys. Sev. Pomegranate. Dahi. Papdi. Crunchy peanuts. Whatever else you want.

Eaten with a spoon. Mostly with hands. Never with a fork.
